## Types of Domains

Lettr supports three types of domains, each requiring different DNS records:

| Domain Type | Purpose | Required Records |
|-------------|---------|------------------|
| **Sending Domain** | Send emails from your domain | CNAME (subdomain) or TXT SPF (primary), DKIM, DMARC |
| **Tracking Domain** | Custom click/open tracking URLs | CNAME for SSL + CNAME for traffic |
| **Inbound Domain** | Receive emails at your domain | MX records |

## Sending Domain Records

Sending domains can be configured in two ways:

### Option 1: Subdomain (Recommended)

Use a subdomain like `sender.example.com` with CNAME verification:

| Type | Host | Value |
|------|------|-------|
| CNAME | sender | sparkpostmail.com |
| TXT | scph0226._domainkey.sender | (provided in dashboard) |
| TXT | _dmarc.sender | v=DMARC1;p=none; |

### Option 2: Primary Domain

Use your root domain with TXT SPF verification:

| Type | Host | Value |
|------|------|-------|
| TXT | @ | v=spf1 include:_spf.sparkpostmail.com ~all |
| TXT | scph0226._domainkey | (provided in dashboard) |
| TXT | _dmarc | v=DMARC1;p=none; |

<Info>
  The DKIM record hostname and value are unique to your domain. You'll find the exact values in your Lettr dashboard.
</Info>

## Tracking Domain Records

Custom tracking domains require two CNAME records:

| Type | Host | Value | Purpose |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| CNAME | _acme-challenge.click | (provided in dashboard) | SSL certificate verification |
| CNAME | click | proxy.lettr-tracking.com | Traffic routing |

<Tip>
  SSL certificates are automatically generated and renewed. All tracking URLs use HTTPS for security.
</Tip>

## Inbound Domain Records

To receive emails at your domain, add MX records:

| Type | Host | Value | Priority |
|------|------|-------|----------|
| MX | inbound | rx1.sparkpostmail.com | 10 |
| MX | inbound | rx2.sparkpostmail.com | 10 |
| MX | inbound | rx3.sparkpostmail.com | 10 |

<Warning>
  We recommend using a subdomain like `inbound.example.com` for receiving emails. Using your root domain's MX records will route ALL email to Lettr, which may interfere with your existing email service.
</Warning>

## Verification Timeline

After adding DNS records, verification typically takes:

- **Cloudflare, Vercel, Netlify**: 1-5 minutes (fast propagation)
- **Most providers**: 15 minutes to 1 hour
- **Slow propagation**: Up to 48 hours in rare cases

You can check DNS propagation status using tools like [WhatsMyDNS](https://www.whatsmydns.net/).
